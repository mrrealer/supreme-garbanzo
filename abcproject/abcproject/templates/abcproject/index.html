Getting A Better Caregiver is as easy as 1 - 2 - 3.

1. Tell Us About Your Needs.

First, we'd like to get to know you and what your needs are. Choose from an array of services rendered professional registered nurses trained in home infusion therapy and homecare, licensed practical nurses, certified home health aides, home health aides, physical therapists, and medical social workers. And to help with complicated and potentially unsafe medication regimens, we even have a medical management program staffed by a licensed pharmacist.

2. Schedule Your Visit

Now that you told us what you need, you just have to let us know when and where you'll need the visit. Simply click the "Get A Better Caregiver" button or call us at 855-5-ABC-NOW, and we will service your request within 24 hours. It's that easy.

3. Confirm Your Visit

After you've told us your needs, confirmed the days, times, and frequency of visit(s), and worked out payment details, you're all set. Sit back and rest assured your family members are going to receive the professional, courteous, and culturally competent care they deserve in a timely manner. You'll receive a phone call from your caregiver within 24 hours of their visit to introduce themselves and confirm logistical details.

Ongoing Training

Background checks, references, and certifications are all good places to start, but a home health agency has to do more to provide a better caregiver. As such, we've pioneered a system that allows us to provide ongoing training to each of our caregivers. We train, track, and train on a semi-daily basis.

No Minimum Wage Workers

Up to 80% our revenue goes directly to the caregiver taking care of your loved one. If we paid minimum wage, we might as well say, "If we could pay you less, we would." That's no way to treat someone with a job as important as taking care of your family; so we don't do it.

We're a Tech-Startup!

Well, not so much. But, like a tech-startup, A Better Caregiver uses technology to increase operational efficiency, but we're not doing it to make a 'bajillion' dollars. Instead, we pass our cost savings onto the caregivers actually taking care of your family members. We do this in the form of increased wages, ongoing training, and advancement opportunities.
